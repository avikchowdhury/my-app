Welcome — Let's Get You Started

Never used an expense tracker before? No problem at all. This guide explains every screen in plain language, tells you why each feature exists, and walks you through exactly what to click — in the right order. Set aside about 15 minutes and you will have everything up and running.

1. Create Your Account & Sign In

Think of this as the front door. Until you create an account and verify your email, nothing else is accessible. It only takes a minute.

First time? Register here

  1. Open the app. You will land on the Login / Register page automatically. Click the Register tab.
  2. Fill in your full name, email address, and a password (at least 8 characters). Your password is stored securely — the app never stores it in plain text.
  3. Click Create Account.
  4. Check your inbox. You will receive a 6-digit code called an OTP (One-Time Password). Type it into the box that appears on screen within 10 minutes.
  5. ✅ What happens next: Your account is confirmed and you are logged in automatically. The app takes you straight to the Dashboard. You will not need to verify again.

Already have an account? Log in

  1. Enter the email and password you registered with.
  2. Click Log In.
  3. ✅ What happens next: You land on the Dashboard. The app remembers your login for 24 hours, so you will not be asked to log in again during that time.
OTP not in your inbox? Check your spam folder first. If it still has not arrived after a minute, click Resend OTP on the verification screen.
Why email verification? It prevents anyone else from registering with your email address and accessing your financial data.
Session expires after 24 h If you come back next day and see the login screen, that is normal — just log in again.

2. First-Time Setup — Do These Three Things First

Before you upload any receipts, spend a few minutes on setup. The AI inside this app learns from the labels and limits you create here. If you skip setup, the AI cannot give you useful advice — so this is the most important part of getting started.

Step A — Create Your Spending Categories

What is a category? A category is simply a label — like a folder — that groups similar expenses together. For example, every time you buy food at a supermarket, that receipt goes into “Groceries”. Every restaurant visit goes into “Dining”. The AI uses these labels to analyse where your money is going.

  1. Click Categories in the left sidebar.
  2. Click + New Category.
  3. Type a name that makes sense to you — common examples: Groceries, Dining, Transport, Housing, Subscriptions, Health, Entertainment, Travel.
  4. Click Save. Repeat for each area of spending you want to track.
  5. ✅ What happens next: Every other part of the app — the receipt uploader, budget planner, charts, and AI — will now offer these categories as options.
Not sure how many to create? Start with 4–6 broad ones. You can always add more later. Having fewer, broader categories is better than dozens of tiny ones.

Step B — Add Vendor Rules (Auto-Tagging)

What is a vendor rule? It is a shortcut. You tell the app “any receipt from Whole Foods belongs in Groceries” — and from then on it happens automatically, every time, with no manual work from you.

  1. Still on the Categories screen, find a category (e.g. Groceries) and click its row to expand it.
  2. Open the Vendor Rules panel and click + Add Rule.
  3. Type the vendor's name or a unique word from it — for example: Whole Foods, Netflix, Uber, Starbucks.
  4. Click Save Rule.
  5. ✅ What happens next: The next time you upload a receipt from that vendor, the category is filled in for you automatically. The more rules you add, the less manual correction you will ever need to do.
Tip: Add rules for the 5–10 places you shop most often. That covers the majority of your receipts immediately.

Step C — Set Monthly Budgets

What is a budget? It is the maximum amount you want to spend in a category per month. For example, you might decide you want to spend no more than $400 on Groceries or $100 on Dining. The app tracks how close you are getting to that limit and warns you before you go over.

  1. Click Budgets in the sidebar.
  2. Click + Add Budget.
  3. Choose a category from the dropdown (the ones you just created) and type your monthly limit — e.g. $500.
  4. Click Save. Repeat for each category you care about.
  5. ✅ What happens next: The Dashboard, Budget Advisor, Forecast page, and the notification bell all now compare your real spending against these limits and alert you when things are drifting.
No budget = no alerts Without a budget set for a category, the AI cannot warn you when you are overspending in it.
Change it anytime If your limit feels too tight or too loose after the first month, just edit it. The AI adjusts instantly.
Don't overthink the number Set a rough estimate for now. After one month of tracking receipts you will have real data to set a smarter limit.

3. The Dashboard — Your Financial Home Screen

After logging in, the Dashboard is the first screen you see. Think of it as a daily health check for your money. You don't have to do anything here — just read and observe. The AI does the analysis for you.

What each part means

  • Budget Health card — a single percentage showing how much of your total monthly budget is already used up. Green is fine, amber means you are approaching your limits, red means you are close to or over budget.
  • Top Category — the one area where you have spent the most money this month. Useful to spot patterns quickly.
  • AI Sync card — click Sync AI to ask the system to re-read all your recent receipts and refresh its advice. Do this once a day for the most up-to-date guidance.
  • Monthly Spend chart — a bar for each day of the month showing how much you spent that day. Tall bars on weekends or specific dates are easy to spot.
  • Category Breakdown chart — a donut showing what percentage of your total spend went to each category. Instantly reveals where most of your money is going.
  • AI Guidance cards — up to three written tips from the AI. Examples: “Dining is 20 % over pace — consider cooking at home this week” or “Your Subscriptions budget is fine.”
  • Export to Excel button — downloads all your receipts as a spreadsheet — useful for sharing with an accountant.

The AI Copilot Chat — ask anything

At the bottom of the Dashboard is a chat panel. Type a question in plain English and the AI answers using your actual spending data. You do not need to know any commands or special words.

"How am I doing this month?" Get a plain-English summary of your spending versus budget.
"What did I spend on dining?" The AI looks up your receipts and gives you the exact total.
"Which budget is most at risk?" Instantly see where you are most likely to overspend before month end.
"How do I add a vendor rule?" Ask the AI for help using the app itself — it knows every feature.

4. Receipts — How to Add Your Expenses

Receipts are the core of the app. Every expense you add — whether uploaded as a photo, a PDF, or typed as plain text — becomes a “receipt” in the system. The more receipts you add, the smarter and more accurate the AI becomes.

Option 1 — Upload a photo or PDF receipt

This is the quickest and most accurate way. Just photograph your paper receipt or attach a digital PDF.

  1. Click Receipts in the sidebar, then click Upload Receipt in the top-right corner.
  2. Select a photo (JPG / PNG) or PDF from your device.
  3. The AI reads the receipt image in seconds and automatically fills in:
    • Vendor name — e.g. “Whole Foods Market”
    • Amount — e.g. $87.45
    • Date — pulled from the receipt itself
    • Category — matched against your vendor rules, or AI's best guess
  4. Look over the filled-in details. If the category is wrong, change it using the dropdown. Everything else is usually correct.
  5. Duplicate check: If you accidentally upload the same receipt twice, a yellow warning banner appears at the top. You can dismiss it and save anyway, or go back and check — your choice.
  6. Click Save.
  7. ✅ What happens next: The receipt is saved. A brief AI note appears explaining what this purchase means for your budgets — for example, “This brings your Groceries spend to 78 % of budget.”

Option 2 — Quick Add (type it in words)

No receipt to hand? No problem. This feature lets you just describe the purchase in plain English and the AI figures out the details.

  1. Click Quick Add (AI) in the Receipts page header.
  2. Type what you spent in your own words. Examples:
    “Starbucks coffee $6.50 this morning”
    “Paid $120 for electricity bill yesterday”
    “Lunch at Subway for $9”
  3. Click Parse. The AI extracts vendor, amount, date, and category.
  4. Review the result and click Save to confirm.
  5. ✅ What happens next: A proper receipt record is created — identical to one uploaded from a photo. Great for cash purchases or when you forget to keep the receipt.

Viewing, editing and deleting receipts

  • Use the search bar at the top of the Receipts page to find a specific purchase by vendor name or amount.
  • Use the date and category filters to narrow down to a time period or spending area.
  • Click any row to see the full receipt details and the original image.
  • Click the pencil icon to edit any field.
  • Click the bin icon to delete (a confirmation box appears first — no accidents).

5. AI Insights — Understand Your Spending Deeply

Once you have uploaded at least a week of receipts, the Insights page becomes very powerful. It is like having a personal finance analyst who reads all your data and writes you a report — but it updates automatically and costs nothing extra.

Click Insights in the sidebar. You will see four tabs.

Tab 1 — Overview (start here)

This tab gives you the big picture. Even if you never look at the other tabs, check this one regularly.

  • AI Narrative — a paragraph written by the AI summarising your month so far in plain English.
  • Risk cards — if any category is heading towards overspend, a coloured card appears here explaining it.
  • Budget Advisor — a row for every budget showing projected end-of-month spend versus your limit. Green = safe, red = over.
  • Budget Coach — one personalised suggestion on the single most useful thing you could change right now (e.g. “Cut two dining visits this week and you will stay under budget”).

Tab 2 — Subscriptions

Many people lose track of how many subscriptions they pay for — this tab finds them all automatically by looking at recurring patterns in your receipts.

  • Each subscription shows its name, monthly cost, and when it will charge next.
  • An AI risk score (1–10) suggests whether the subscription is worth keeping. A score of 8–10 means it charges a lot or is used rarely.
  • A calendar at the bottom shows upcoming charges in the next 30 days so there are no billing surprises.

Tab 3 — Anomalies

An anomaly is when you spend significantly more than usual in a category. The AI compares this month to your own history and flags anything unusual.

  • Each card shows which category spiked, by how much in dollars, and what percentage above normal it is.
  • A written explanation helps you understand whether the spike is a one-off or a trend to watch.

Tab 4 — Vendors

Ever wonder which single shop or service takes the most of your money? This tab tells you.

  • A grid of cards — one per vendor — shows your total spend, number of visits, and whether spending at that vendor went up or down versus last month (↑ or ↓).
  • An AI Observation at the top notes patterns — for example if you are heavily concentrated with one vendor or if a vendor's cost has risen significantly.

Download the AI Report (Excel)

Click the AI Report button at the top right of the Insights page. You get a 3-tab Excel file:

Sheet 1 — Summary Budget pace, top categories, and key monthly metrics at a glance.
Sheet 2 — Receipts A clean table of every receipt — great to share with an accountant or for tax records.
Sheet 3 — AI Insights The AI narrative, anomaly list, and vendor notes exported as readable text.

6. Forecast — See the Future Before It Happens

Most people only realise they overspent when the month is already over. The Forecast screen shows you where you are heading right now — while you still have time to change it.

Click Forecast in the sidebar to open this screen.

  • Projected Total — based on how much you have spent so far this month, the AI calculates what your full-month total will be if you carry on at the same pace.
  • Over or under budget? — a coloured indicator immediately tells you whether the projection is within your combined budget (green), approaching the limit (amber), or heading over (red).
  • Daily bar chart — the left half of the chart shows what you actually spent each day. The right half (lighter bars) shows what the AI predicts for the remaining days. Hover any bar to see the exact amount.
  • AI Narrative — a sentence or two explaining the forecast. For example: “Your Dining spend is the main driver. Reducing it by $30 this week would bring you under budget.”
New to the app? The forecast is most reliable from day 7 onwards. In the first few days of the month it has little data so the projection will be rough — that is completely normal.

7. Notification Bell — Alerts Without Checking Every Screen

The bell icon in the top-right corner of every page is your passive safety net. It watches your spending for you in the background and only interrupts you when something genuinely needs attention.

When the bell has a red number badge, it means there are unread alerts. Click it to open the panel.

What will trigger an alert?

💰 Budget warning A category has crossed 80 % or 100 % of its monthly limit — time to slow down spending there.
⚠️ Spending spike A category jumped significantly above your usual level this month — worth checking if it's expected.
🔄 Subscription due soon A recurring charge (like Netflix or a gym membership) is expected to hit within the next 3 days.
  1. Click the bell to open the notification panel.
  2. Read each alert — the icon type tells you whether it is a budget, anomaly, or subscription alert.
  3. Click Mark all read to clear the badge once you have reviewed them.

8. Budgets — Track and Adjust Your Limits

You set your first budgets during setup. This section explains what the full Budgets screen shows you day-to-day and how to adjust things if your limits feel wrong.

Click Budgets in the sidebar.

  • Budget cards — each category budget gets its own card. It shows the monthly limit you set, how much you have spent so far this month, how much is remaining, and a progress bar. When the bar turns red you are close to the limit.
  • Budget Advisor — below the cards, the AI writes one sentence for each budget. Examples: “Groceries on pace — no action needed,” or “Housing is 90 % used with 12 days remaining — risk of overspend.”
  • Edit a budget — click the pencil icon on any card to change the monthly limit. Click Save and the AI updates immediately.
  • Delete a budget — click the bin icon. This removes the limit and the alerts for that category only. Your receipts in that category are not deleted.
Budget card shows $0 spent? This usually means the receipts in that category are labelled with a slightly different name. Go to Receipts, filter by that category, and check — if you see the right receipts there, everything is working correctly.

9. Categories & Vendor Rules — Fine-Tuning Over Time

After a few weeks of using the app you will notice patterns — maybe you want to split “Dining” into “Lunch” and “Dinner,” or you notice a vendor is always miscategorised. This screen is where you fix all of that.

Click Categories in the sidebar.

  • Rename a category — click the pencil icon next to any category name and type the new name. All receipts assigned to it automatically update to the new name.
  • Delete a category — the app warns you if receipts are still using it. Reassign those receipts first, then delete.
  • Add a new vendor rule — expand any category row, open Vendor Rules, and click + Add Rule. Type the vendor's name (or a keyword from it). From now on that vendor's receipts are tagged automatically.
  • Delete a vendor rule — click the bin icon next to any rule. Future receipts from that vendor will no longer be auto-tagged, but existing receipts are not changed.
Pro tip: Each time you manually correct a receipt's category, add a vendor rule for that vendor right after. You will never have to correct that vendor again.

10. Profile — Your Personal Settings

This is where you manage your own account — your name, photo, and password. Changes here appear across the whole app instantly.

Click your name or avatar in the sidebar to open Profile, or use the Profile menu item.

  1. Update your name or bio — click Edit Profile, type your name and an optional short bio, then click Save.
  2. Upload a profile photo (avatar) — click the circle with your initials (top of the profile page). Choose a JPG or PNG from your device (max 5 MB). It uploads instantly and appears in the sidebar navigation too.
  3. Change your password — scroll to the Security section. Enter your current password and type the new one twice for confirmation. Click Update Password. ✅ You will stay logged in — no need to log out.

11. Admin Panel (only visible to Admin accounts)

Most users will never see this screen. It only appears in the sidebar if your account has been given the Admin role by another administrator. It is for managing the whole workspace — not individual expense tracking.
  • User list — see every registered account, their email, and how many receipts they have uploaded.
  • Assign or remove the Admin role — toggle the Admin switch on any user. Admins can see workspace statistics, so only grant this to people who need it.
  • Workspace stats — three summary cards at the top showing total registered users, total receipts across all accounts, and total spend tracked in the system.

12. Your Recommended Weekly Routine

Once setup is done, it only takes about 5 minutes a week to keep everything accurate and get the most value from the AI. Here is the routine that works best.
  1. Upload this week's receipts — use the photo upload for paper receipts and Quick Add for anything you paid in cash or forgot to keep. The more complete your data, the better the AI's advice.
  2. Fix any wrong categories — go to Receipts, look for rows with a missing or incorrect category, and correct them. Then add a vendor rule so it doesn't happen again.
  3. Check the notification bell — read any alerts and mark them as read. If a budget alert fired, decide if you need to slow spending or adjust the limit.
  4. Open the Dashboard and hit Sync AI — read the latest guidance cards. They update every time you sync.
  5. Glance at Forecast — a 10-second check to confirm you are still on track to finish the month within budget.
  6. Ask the Copilot one question — finish with something like “What should I cut this week?” or “Am I on track for the month?”

13. Common Questions & Quick Fixes

Receipt photo didn't parse correctly Use a clear, well-lit photo with the text facing the camera. PDF receipts (from online orders) work even better than photos.
AI put the wrong category on a receipt Edit the receipt to fix it, then immediately add a vendor rule for that merchant — it will never be wrong again.
Got a duplicate warning but the purchase is real The app found a very similar receipt from the past 7 days. Click "Dismiss warning" and save — the receipt will be saved normally.
Budget card shows £0 / $0 spent Receipts may be in a differently-named category. Go to Receipts, filter by that category name, and check if any show up.
Forecast seems way off It needs at least 5–7 days of data to be reliable. In the first few days of a new month it will extrapolate from very little — check back mid-month.
Need a report for your accountant or tax return Go to Insights → click "AI Report". The Excel download includes a full receipt list with dates, vendors, categories, and amounts.
